I am a robotics graduate student at Georgia Tech with a background in electrical engineering,
autonomous systems, RF research, and hands-on robotic systems integration. My work has ranged
from award-winning undergraduate research and conference publications to student-team leadership,
field testing, and production engineering in industry.

## Education

**M.S. in Robotics, Georgia Institute of Technology (Fall 2024 - Present).**
Focused on autonomous systems, state estimation, deep reinforcement learning, and sim-to-real
deployment for real-world robotic platforms.

**B.S. in Electrical Engineering, Georgia Institute of Technology (Fall 2021 - Spring 2024).**
Completed in three years. Academic and research work spanned RF systems, robotics, controls,
and embedded/autonomous platforms. Highlights included a semester in France working with Dr. David
Citrin at Georgia Tech-CNRS IRL 2958 on terahertz (THz) imaging on paper, a summer research
internship at the University of California San Diego's Scripps Institution of Oceanography, the
President's Undergraduate Research Award, best poster recognition at IEEE DTPI 2023, and conference
publications and presentations through IEEE ORSS 2023 and IEEE OCEANS 2025.

## Activities

Beyond coursework and research, I have stayed active through leadership and mentoring in the
Georgia Tech Marine Robotics Group, as well as competition-oriented systems work supporting
RoboBoat, RoboSub, RobotX, and the Stinger Tug educational platform.
